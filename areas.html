Computation of Areas

In surveying finding of area of the plot plays a major role.It has to be done accurately.

Introduction :

The term 'area' in the context of surveying refers to the area of a tract of land projected upon the horizontal plane,and not to the actual area of the land surface.

Area may be expressed in the following units:

  1. Square-metres
  2. Hectares(1 hectare = 10000 m2)
  3. Square-feet
  4. Acres(1 acre = 4840 sq.yd.=43.560 sq.ft.)

The following is a hierarchical representation of the various methods of computation of area.

Computation of Area from field notes

This is done in two steps.

Step 1 :

In cross-staff survey,the area of field can be directly calculated from field notes. During survey work the whole area is divided into some geometrical figures,such aside triangles,rectangles,squares and trapeziums and then the area is calculated as follows :
  1. Area of triangle
    = √(s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c))
    2. where a,b and c are the sides.
    and s =(a+b+c)/2
    Area of triangle = ½×b×h
    where b = base
    h=altitude
  2. Area of Rectangle = a × b
    3. where a and b are the sides.
  3. Area of Square = a2
    4. where a is the side of the square.
  4. Area of trapezium =½(a+b)×d
    5. where a and b are the parallel sides,and d is the perpendicula distance between them.

Step 2 :

D = (o1+o2)/2
Area of shaded portion = D ×(x1-x2)

Similarly,the areas between all pairs of ordinates are calculated and added to obtain the total boundary area.Hence

Total area of the Field

Computation of Area From Plotted Plan

The area may be calculated in the two following ways.

Case 1 - Considering the entire area

The entire area is divided into regions of a convenient shape , and calculated as follows :

  1. By dividing the area into triangles

    2. The triangles are so drawn as to equalise the irregular boundary line.

  2. By dividing the area into squares

    3. In this method,squares of equal size are ruled out on a piece of tracing paper.Each square represents a unit area,which could be 1 cm2 or 1 m2.The tracing paper is placed over the plan and the number of full squares are counted.The total area

  3. By drawing parallel lines and converting them to rectangles

    4. Thus the area is divided into a number of strips.The curved ends of the strips are placed perpendicular lines and a number of rectangles are formed.The sum of the lengths of the rectangles is then calculated.

    Then,
    Required area = Σlength of rectangles ×constant distance

    Case 2:

    In this method,a large square or rectangle is formed within the area in the plan. Then ordinates are drawn at regular intervals from the side of the square to the curved boundary. The middle area is calculated in the usual way.The boundary area is calculated according to one of the following rules:

  1. The mid-ordinate rule
  2. The average ordinate rule
  3. The trapezoidal rule
  4. Simpson's rule

The various rules are explained in the following sections.

  1. The Mid-ordinate rule :

    2. Consider the following figure,

    Mid Ordinate

    Area of plot =
    h1×d + h2×d + ......+ hn×d
    i.e., Area =
    common distance × sum of mid-ordinates
  2. The Average-ordinate rule :

    3. Average-Ordinate

    Let   O1,O2,O3,.....On = ordinates at equal intervals
    l = length of base line
    n = number of divisions
    n+1 = number of ordinates
    Area = {(O1+O2+O3......+On)/(n+1)}×l
    i.e.,
    Area = (sum of ordinates/no.of ordinates)×length of base line
  3. The Trapezoidal rule :

    4. Trapezoidal Rule

    While applying the trapezoidal rule,boundaries between the ends of ordinates are assumed to be straight.Thus the area enclosed between the base line and the irregular boundary line are considered as trapezoids.

    Let   O1,O2,O3,.....On = ordinates at equal intervals
    1st area = ((O1+O2)/2)×d
    2nd area = ((O2+O3)/2)×d
    Total area = d(O1+2O1+2O2......+2On-1+On)/2
    Limitation::There is no limitaion for this rule.This rule can be applied for any number of ordinates.
  4. Simphson rule:

    5. Simphson Rule

    In this rule,the boundaries between the ends of ordinates are assumed to form an arc of a parabola.Hence Simpson's rule is sometimes called the Simpson's rule.



    Here,  

    Area of trapezium = ((O1+O2)/2)×2d

    Area of Segment = 2/3×(area of parallelogram)

    = 2/3×(Ee×2d)=2/3×{O2-((O1+O3)/2)}×2d)

    So,the area between the first two divisions,

    Δ1=[{(O1+O2)/2}×2d]+2/3[{O2-(O1+O3)/2)}×2d]

       = d/3(O1+4O2+O3)

    Similarly,the area between next two divisions.

    Δ2=d/3(O3+4O4+O5)      and so on.
    Total area = d/3(O1+4O2+2O3+.......+On)
    = d/3{O1+On+4(O2+O4+....)
    +2(O3+O5)}
    Limitation::This rule is applicable only when the number divisions is even i.e., number of ordinates is odd.
