Chain surveying

Chain surveying is one of the methods of land surveying.Chain surveying is the simplest and at the same time most accurate method of surveying.In this type of surveying only linear measurements are taken in the field, with the help of chain,tape and offset rod.

Suitable situations :

  1. The area of land to be small
  2. The ground is fairly level and covering with simple details
  3. The formation of well conditioned triangle is easy

Unsuitable situations :

  1. The area of land to be surveyed is large
  2. The area of land is undulated
  3. The formation of well conditioned triangles becomes difficullt due to obstacles like large buildings.

Purpose of Chain surveying

  1. To fix the boundaries of the land .
  2. To calculate the area of the plot.
  3. To collect data required for engineering works,such as roads,railways etc.,

Principles of Chain surveying

The principles of chain surveying is triangulation.When it is done with a chain it is called Chain triangulation.According to this principle,the area to be surveyed is d.ivided into number of small triangles,connected with each other.

Offset:

The lateral measurement taken from an object to the survey line is known as offset.
Offsets are of two types

  1. Perpendicular offset
  2. Oblique offset

Perpendicular Offset:

If the lateral measurement taken right angles to the survey line,they are known as Perpendicular offset.

Oblique Offset:

Offsets not perpendicular to the chain line is set to be oblique offsets.These offsets are taken in the objects are located at a long distance from the chain line.

Chain:

Chains are prepared of galvanised mild steel wire straight links bend into rings at the end and joined each other by three small circular or oval wire rings.The ends of the chain and provided with brass handle at each end with swivel joint,so that the chain can be turned without twisting.
The length of the link is the distance between the centres of two consecutive middle rings.The length of the chain is outside of one handle to the outside of the other handle.

Types of chain:

  1. Metric chain
  2. Gunter's chain or surveyor's chain
  3. Engineer's chain
  4. Revenue chain
  5. Steel band or band chain
  1. Metric chain :

    2. Metric chain are available in lengths of 5,10,20 and 30 meters. Among the 20m and 30m are commonly used. 20m chain is divided into 100 links. Length of each link is 0.2m. The brass tallies are fixed at every 5m length and small brass rings are provided at every metre length.


  2. Gunter's chain or Surveyor's chain:

    3. This chain is 66 feet long and having 100 links. So, each is of 0.66 ft. It is very useful for measuring the distances in miles and furlongs. It is also useful for measuring the area in acres.

    1. 1 Gunter's chain = 22 yards
    2. 10 Gunter's chain = 1 furlong
    3. 8o Gunter's chain = 1 mile
    4. 1 Square gunter chain =484 sq. yards(10 cents).
    5. 10 Square gunter chain = 1 Acre = 4840 sq. yards (100 cents)
  3. Engineer's chain :

    4. The engineer's chain is 100 ft long and divided into 100 links. So, length of each link is of 1 foot. Tallies or tags are provided at every 10 links (10 ft.), the cetral tally being round. Engineer's chain was previously used for all engineering works.

  4. Revenue chain :

    5. This chain is 33 ft. long and divided into 16 links. Length of each link being 2 ft 0.75 inches.

  5. Steel band or band chain :

    6. It consists of a ribbon of steel of width 16mm and of length 20 or 30 m. It has brass handle at both ends.

Arrows :

Arrows are made of good quality hardened and tempered steel wire of diameter 4 mm. The overall length of the arrow may be 400 mm one end of the arrow is bent into a ring of 50mm. diametre and other end is made sharp pointed.

Tapes :

Tapes are used to take more accurate measurements.
They are classified according to the material in which they are made:

  1. Cloth or linen tape
  2. Metallic tape
  3. Steel tape
  4. Invar tape

Cloth or linen tape:

It is tape made up of closely woven linen and is varnished to resist moisture. It is 12 to 15 mm wide and available in length of 10,20 and 30 meters.

Metallic tape

If a linen tape is reinforced with eith brass,copper or bronze wires to a make durable,then it is called a metallic tape.In this tape the wires are provided to avoid stretching.These tapes are available in lengths of 2,5,10,20 and 30m.

Steel tape:

The steel tape is made of steel ribbon of width varying from 6 to 16 mm wide. These tapes are available in lengths of 1,2,5,10,20,30 and 50m. It is graduated in meters,decimeters,centimeters and millimetres.

Invar tape:

It is made of an alloy of steel (64%) and nickel (36%). Its coefficient of thermal expansion is very low,hence it is not affected by change in temparature.These are 6 mm wide and available in length 30 ,50 and 100 m.It is costly and delicate.

Ranging Rods:

Ranging rods are circular or octagonalin cross section of 30 mm diametre and having a length of 2 or 3 m.Ranging rods are used for locating station points and for ranging a survey line.

Offset Rods:

An offset rod is similar to a ranging rod and has a length of 3m.Generally they are round wooden rods,having a iron shoe at bottom and a notch or hook at the top.

Cross-Staff or Optical Square:

  1. Cross staff :

    2. The cross-staff is a simple instrument used for setting-out right angles.It consists of either a frame or box with two pairs of vertical slits and is mounted on a iron pole shoed for fixing into the ground.there are two types of cross stave.

    • Open cross staff
    • French cross staff
    • Wooden cross staff
  2. Wooden Cross staff:

    3. It consists of a square wooden block mounted on a iron pole having a sharp edge at the bottom.For the wooden block grooves are cut upto the half thickness in perpendicular directions. In this cross staff all the slits can be used to set perpendiculars. The working os this cross staff in similar to open cross-staff.

Plumb-Bob:

It is used to check the verticality of ranging rods and to transfer points from a line ranger or opyical square.It is also used for centering of the surveying instruments such as compass, theodolite,tachometer and plane-table.It is generally made up of Iron or Brass.

Wooden-Pegs:

The wooden-pegs are used to a mark the positions of the survey stations. They are made up of hard timber.They ae made 2 to 4 cm squqre or 2 to 5 cm diameter and 15 to 20 cm long, tapered at the bottom end.They are driven into the ground with the help of a wooden hammer and kept projected about 5 cm above the surface of ground.

Mallet:

Mallet or wooden hammer generally used in carpentary work,is used for driving pegs into the ground .It is made from hard wood 8 to 10 cm in diametre.

Ranging of survey lines:

The process of establishing intermediate points on a survey line,between two stations is known as ranging of survey lines or simply ranging. Ranging must be done before chaining a survey line.

Direct Ranging:

The ranging of a survey line done by direct observation from end stations,when the end stations are intervisible,is called Direct Ranging. The direct ranging is carried out by an eye or with line ranger.

  1. Ranging by Eye:

    2. The ranging with eye is carried out by following steps

    • A and B are two at the ends of survey line.
    • erect ranging rods at stations A and B.
    • The surveyor standing 1.5 to 2 m behind the ranging rod at A,directs the assistant to put a ranging rod approximately in line,between A and B.
    • The surveyor standing behind A,signals the assistant to move left or right until three ranging rods are exactly in a straight line.
    • The surveyor check yhe vertically of the rods by sighting the lower ends of the rods.
    • Similarly,ranging is continued to put required no.of intermediate points,in between A and B.
  2. Ranging by Line Ranger:

    3. Line ranger is an opticalinstrument used for fixing intermediate points on a chain line. A line ranger consists of either two plane mirrors or two right angled isoscales prisms placed one above the other.The diagonals of the two prisms are silvered so as to reflect the incidental rays.A handle with a hook is provided at the bottom to hold the instrument in hand and to transfer the point on to the ground with the help of plumb-bob.

    Procedure:

    • To fix an intermediate station 'p' on yhe line AB,the observer stands as near P as possible and holds the instrument as his eye level.
    • A ray of light from A enters the prism and gets reflected from the hypotenuse ac,and visible to the surveyor at right angles to the AB.
    • Similarly another light ray from B enters the lower pism at a time.
    • If the point 'p' is not in line of AB two images will be seen seperated .
    • The surveyor then moves the instrument side ways till the two images are visible in the same vertical line.
    • The point 'p' is then transferred to the ground with the help of a plumb-bob.
    • Hence,the instrument can be conveniently used for fixing intermediate points on a survey line, without going to their end.

    Indirect or Reciprocal Ranging:

    When both the end survey stations are not intervisible either due to high ground in between them or due to a long distance between them,the ranging is done by indirect or Reiprocal ranging.

    procedure:

    • A and B are two end stations,which are not intervisible due to a hill in between them.
    • Select two intermediate points P1 and Q1,such that from each station point A and B are visible.
    • Two persons take up the positions P1 and Q1 with ranging rods.
    • First the person standing at P1 directs the person at Q1 to come in the line of P1B,and his new position will be Q2.
    • Now,the person standing at Q2,directs the person at P1,to come in line of Q2A, and his new position will be P2.
    • Now,the person standing at P2,directs the person at Q2,to come in line of P2B,and his new position will be Q3.
    • This process is continued until the intermediate points P and Q are located in such a way that the person standing at P.see Q and B in the line.
    • Finally,the accuracy of work is checked from A andB.

    Unfolding and Folding a Chain

    Unfolding a chain:
    • Before taking measurements the chain should be unfolded.
    • To unfold a chain,the strap is unfastened.
    • Now,the chain is lifted into the right hand,keeping brass handles in left hand,the chain should be thrown with right hand.
    • Then follower stands at the starting station by holding one handle and leader moves forward by holding the other handle until the chain is completely opened.
    • Now,the chain is checked for correct length by observing if there is any links or bent in links.

    Folding a chain:
    • After the days work is completed the chain should be folded and fastened with a leaner strap.
    • To fold a chain,the two handles are to be brought to one end by pulling the chain at middle.
    • After this,commencing from the middle of the chain,two pairs of links are taken at a time with the right hand and placed on the left hand alternatively in both directions.
    • Finally,the two brass handles will appear at the top.The bunch should be then fastened with the leather strap

Method of chaining on Sloping Ground

Chaining on sloping ground gives the sloping distance.But for all plotting works,horizontal distances between the points are required. Hence,it is necessary either to directly measure the horizontal distances between the points or to measure the sloping distance and calculate its horizontal equivalent.Thus,there are two methods for determining horizontal distance on sloping ground

  1. Direct method or stepping method
  2. indirect method

    Direct method or stepping method:

    This method is applied when the slope of the griund is very steep.In this method,te sloping ground is divided into number of horizontal and vertical strips,like steps.The lengths of the horizontal portions are measured with chain or tape and added to get the total horizontal distance between the points.The steps may not be uniform,and they would depend on the nature of the ground.

    procedure:
    1. Let it is required to find the horizontal distance between points A and B
    2. First,the line AB is ranged properly.
    3. The follower holds the zero end of the tape at A and the leader select a suitable length AP1(l1),so that P1 is about chest level.
    4. The horizontally of line AP1 is maintained by eye estimation or by tri-square.
    5. The point P2 is marked on the ground with the plumb-bob,such that P1 is just over P2 and the horizontal length 'l1' is noted.
    6. Then the follower moves the position P2 and holds zero end of the tape at P2.
    7. Again the leader select a suitable length P2P3(l2),the point P4 is marked on to the ground,such that P4 is just over P3 and the horizontal length 'l2' is noted.
    8. Similarly,the horizontal length 'l3' is measured and noted The total horizontal length,
    AB = L = AP1 + P2P3 + P4P5 = l1 + l2 + l3

    Indirect method:

    This method is adopted when the slope of the ground surface is uniform and the stepping method is not suitable.In this case the horizontal distance can be calculated by measuring the slope distance and the angle of slope or the difference in elevation betweenthe points.To calculate the horizontal distance indirectly,the following methods can be applied.

    1. By measuring the angle of slope with clinometer.
    2. By measuring the difference in level with a levelling instrument and
    3. By applying hypotensual allowance.

Correction due to Incorrect length of chain or Tape:

Due to continuous use of chain over ground,a chain may be elongated or shortened. If we take measurements with these chains,the values obtained are wrong.This can be avoided by testing and adjusting the chain.The chains are tested with the help of standard steel tape.
If full adjustment is not possible,then the amount of elongation or the amount of shortening should be noted clearly.
Correction to the measurements taken by using incorrect chain or tape is applied by using the following formulae. 

1.Correction is applied to incorrect length of chain or tape.
      True length of line (TL)=L'/L X measured lenth(ML)
       where   L=standard or correct length of the chain
              L'=Incorrect length of chain
                =true length+error (or) true length - error
                =L + e  (or) L - e.
 Use the positive sign when the chain or tape is too long,
the negative sign  when it is too short.

2.Correction to incorrect area.

         True area=(L'/L)2 X measured area.

3.Correction to incorrect volume

       True  volume=(L'/L)3 X measured volume.

Obstacles in chain surveying:

The objects,which create obstruction or difficulty in continuing the chaining of a survey line,are called obstacles. There are many obstacles which create obstruction to the chaining or ranging ,they may be pond,hill,river,valley,building etc., Various methods are adopted to overcome these obstacles and to measure the distance,crossing obstructions.
The obstacles may be classified as follows:

  1. Chaining is obstructed, but vision is free
  2. Chaining is free,but vision is obstructed, and
  3. Chaining and vision,both are obstructed.

Chaining is obstructed,but vision is free :

Such a problem arises when a pond or a river comes across the chain line. The problem may be solved and chaining can be continued in the following ways.

Case 1:

When a pond create obstruction to chaining,it is possible to go around the obstruction.

Method 1: If AB is the chain line,two points P and Q selected on the chain line,on either side of the obstacle. Equal perpendiculars PR and QS are errected at P and Q.The distance RS is measured.Then PQ=RS.

Method 2: A perpendicular PR is erected at point 'p' such that 'Q' can be chained from 'R'.The distance PR and QR are measured .Now,the distance PQ is given by

PQ = sqrt ( QR2 - PR2 )

Case 2:

When a river creates obstruction to chaining ,it is not possible to go round the obstruction.

Method 1:If asmall river comes across the chain line.Let AB is the chain line.Two points P and Q are selected on opposite banks of the river .At P a perpendicular PR is erected and bisected at T.A perpendicular is set out at R and a point S is so selected on it that S, T and Q are in the same straight line.
From triangles PQT and TRS,
PQ=RS
Measure RS, and the distance PQ is obtained indirectly.

Method 2:Consider the case when a large river interrepts the chain line.
Let AB be the chain line,points O,P and Q are selected on this line such that P and Q are on opposite banks of the river.The perpendiculars PR and OS are erected on the chain line in such a way that Q , R and S are on the same straight line.

The line RT is taken parellel to OP.
Now,from similar triangles PQR and TRS,

PQ/PR=RT/ST where RT=OP
PQ=RT/ST ×PR
OT=PR
PQ=OP/(OS-PR) ×PR
ST=OS-OT

The distances OP,OS,PR are measured. Thus the required distance PQ can be calculated.

This type of problem comes,when a rising ground or a forest area interrupts the chain line. Here the end station are not intervisible There may be two cases of this type of obstacle.

Case 1:

Both the ends of the chain line may be visible from intermediate points on the line. In this case,reciprocal ranging is adopted and chaining is done by stepping ranging is adopted and chaining is done by stepping method.

The end stations are not visible from intermediate points when a jungle comes across the chain line.In this case,the obstacle can be crossed over by using a random line as explained.
